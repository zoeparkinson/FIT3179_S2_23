FIFA Women's World Cup 1991-2023

Background

The FIFA Women's World Cup is an international soccer tournament held every four years, featuring the world's top female national teams. Established in 1991, the tournament has grown in popularity and significance, showcasing the skill and athleticism of women in the sport. The competition has provided a platform for female athletes to shine on the global stage and has contributed to the growth of women's soccer worldwide.

Participation

Over the years, the FIFA Women's World Cup has witnessed a significant increase in participation. Beginning with just 12 teams, it has expanded to include 32 in 2023, representing nations from across the globe. The United States of America has played the most matches at 53, with Germany and Sweden tied for second at 47. Many countried have yet to participate in a FIFA Women's World Cup.


Champions

Over the span of the FIFA Women's World Cup from 1991 to 2023, there have been a number of champions. The United States of America asserted their dominance, clinching the title in 1991, 1999, 2015 and 2019. Norway celebrated a victory in 1995, Germany became champions in both 2003 and 2007, and Japan stunned the world with their win in 2011. In 2023, Spain took the title for the very first time.
